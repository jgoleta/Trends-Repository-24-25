Toyota Supra Mk4


The toyota supra is one of the most iconic cars. First appeared in the movie “Fast and the Furious. Supra: Meaning “Transcending” or “Above” in latin, and a rather fitting name for a car which is so much higher than the rest. The Toyota Supra MK4 started production in 1993 in Japan.

The Toyota Supra MK4 is equipped with a twin-turbo 3.0-litre straight-six engine, producing up to 320 hp and around 440 Nm of torque, known as the 2JZ-series. This car accelerates from 0 to 100 km/h in about 4.9 seconds, with a top speed of up to 277 km/h.


Nissan R34 GT-R

The 10th generation Skyline R34 was rolled out in 1998. The body had high rigidity to improve motion performance while securing enough interior space in a smaller body. Its highest-level performance model "GT-R" was released in 1999. "V⋅spec" was the center of talks due to higher traveling performance achieved by exclusive aero parts, specifically tuned suspensions and active LSD, and styling like a racing car.


Toyota AE86

The Toyota AE86, often referred to simply as the “Hachi-Roku,” is one of the most beloved and iconic vehicles in automotive history. With its lightweight design, rear-wheel-drive layout, and impressive handling capabilities, it quickly became a symbol of the golden era of Japanese sports cars. Although the AE86 was produced for only a few years in the mid-1980s, its legacy continues to influence car enthusiasts and motorsport enthusiasts alike. This article delves into the Toyota AE86’s history, performance, design, and cultural impact, showcasing why it remains an iconic figure even decades after its release.


Mitsubishi Evolution VIII

In 2003, the Japanese automobile manufacturer Mitsubishi introduced their quickest and most advanced Evolution. The car was known as the Lancer Evolution VIII and came equipped with cutting-edge technology such as Super Active Yaw Control (AYC).
Its performance was even more impressive than its predecessor, the Lancer Evolution VII. The enhanced performance primarily came from the AYC and other electrical components controlling the power distribution to all four wheels.


Mazda RX-7

The introduction of the first turbocharged rotary engine in the Luce/Cosmo, a similar, also fuel injected and non-intercooled 12A turbocharged engine was made available for the top-end model of the Series 3 RX-7 in Japan. It was introduced in September 1983.The engine was rated at 165 PS (121 kW) (JIS) at 6,500 rpm.

While the peak power figures were only slightly higher than those of the engine used in the Luce/Cosmo, the new "Impact Turbo" was developed specifically to deal with the different exhaust gas characteristics of a rotary engine. Both rotor vanes of the turbine were remodeled and made smaller, and the turbine had a twenty percent higher speed than a turbo intended for a conventional engine.The Savanna Turbo was short-lived, as the next generation of the RX-7 was about to be introduced.


Honda Civic Type-R

Long considered forbidden fruit in the U.S., the Honda Civic Type R made its officially sanctioned stateside debut for the 2017 model year. Since then, it's remained one of the most desirable cars among spirited drivers for its blend of performance and drivability. The current-gen Civic Type R debuted in 2023 with slightly less dramatic styling and cropped aerodynamic plumage, but with an eager turbo four under the hood churning 315 horsepower through a six-speed manual we paid little notice.

Still a track-day toy with hellacious grip, it slips into daily driver duty effortlessly, fulfilling the single-car objective for enthusiasts at a competitive price, putting it squarely on our 2025 10Best list. It competes against the even more feral Toyota GR Corolla both aimed squarely at the elder statesmen of the segment the VW Golf R. All three approach the uncomfortable $50,000 mark, but considering the cost of infusing a standard Civic with Type R performance hardware, well, perhaps the Type R is a bargain in disguise. As one of our favorites in its segment, the Honda Civic Type R has also earned a spot on our Editors’ Choice list for 2025.
